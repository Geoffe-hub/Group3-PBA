6.6. Magnitude Earthquake in Masbate (by: Cidneey Jane Teodoro)

In the province of Masbate in the Bicol region a 6.6 magnitude earthquake had struck at exactly 8:03 am on August 18, 2020. It was felt in the entire Bicol region and in several provinces in the Visayas. The epicenter was located seven kilometers southwest of Cataingan town at a depth of 21 kilometres and was followed by over 210 aftershocks with a maximum of 5.1-magnitude on August 19, 2020.At least 170 people were injured, while 2 were confirmed dead; one retired policeman died after getting trapped when his house collapsed in Cataingan and one had a heart attack. Roads, bridges, and other government infrastructure were damaged and according to the Office of Civil Defense the earthquake destroyed P309 million worth of infrastructure.

How do earthquakes occur?

Earthquakes are usually caused when rock underground suddenly breaks along a fault. This sudden release of energy causes the seismic waves that make the ground shake. When two blocks of rock or two plates are rubbing against each other, they stick a little. And,When the rocks break, the earthquake occurs.

Why do earthquakes occur?

An earthquake is caused by a sudden slip on a fault. When the stress on the edge overcomes the friction, there is an earthquake that releases energy in waves that travel through the earth's crust and cause the shaking that we feel.

We cannot prevent earthquakes from occurring but there are some steps that we can do for us to be prepared if earthquakes will occur.Here are five ways to lessen/minimize the effects of earthquakes:

  • Educate ourselves and our family members.
  • We must learn to practice the duck,cover,and hold.
  • We must prepare disaster supplies( Emergency food and water, Flashlights, First aid kits,Non Electric can opener,Essential medicines,Cash and credit cards, and etc.)
  • Construct buildings that can withstand the force of an earthquake.
  • Have earthquake drills in areas that are prone to earthquakes.

Effectivity of the solutions

  • Educate ourselves and our family members.
    • People who most survive in earthquakes are the ones who know what to do so educating ourselves to learn more about earthquake risks and how to be prepared can help us to save ourselves and others .
  • We must learn to practice the duck,cover,and hold.
    • Drop, Cover, Hold this can help us to avoid being knocked over, and this can help us to protect our head, neck and vital organs.
  • We must prepare disaster supplies( Emergency food and water, Flashlights, First aid kits,Non Electric can opener,Essential medicines,Cash and credit cards, and etc.)
    • Having an emergency kit is an important step to prepare and protect your household for unforeseen events. An emergency kit is essential for short term survival providing vital items for you, your family or household.
  • Construct buildings that can withstand the force of an earthquake.
    • Buildings in earthquake-prone areas need to be built on roller foundations, which allow them to move but not collapse during an earthquake.
  • Have earthquake drills in areas that are prone to earthquakes.
      • The purpose of conducting an earthquake drill is to assist personnel to know their roles and responsibilities in an emergency situation in accordance with the workplace plans and procedures. This helps people to know what they're going to do once an earthquake will happen.
